Unit 7: Historical Narratives

Henry Adams was a prominent historian and novelist during the late 19th century, known for his intricate explorations of the American political landscape and the rapid transition into the industrial age. In this week's primary source analysis, we examine how authors use descriptive imagery to establish a sense of place within a historical context.

By scrutinizing the syntax and tone of these early narratives, students can begin to identify the underlying themes of progress and tradition that defined the era. It is essential to recognize that a narrative is not merely a sequence of events, but a curated perspective designed to evoke a specific emotional response from the audience.
